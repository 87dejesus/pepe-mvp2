What changed and why

  • The core fix, a trust row under the button: ✓ Free · ✓ No credit card · ✓ We only email your code. The live page never states any of this. A Reddit visitor who just saw one free result reads "email" + "send me the code" as the moment they get charged or spammed. These three UX facts kill that fear. (Sanctioned by the copy rule: reassure with UX facts, never expose the revenue model.)
  • Lede reframed from "come back" to "see now": "Sign in so your list is here when you come back" sells account persistence (weak). "Enter your email and I'll send a code to open your full list" sells the thing they want right now.
  • "Takes a minute" removed: it signals delay. Replaced with "lands in a few seconds" plus a plain no-spam / no-selling line.
  • No new promises: we do not say "matches by email" (that follow-up isn't built). No fake urgency, no card, no business-model language. Serif headline, single green CTA, all within the design system.
  • Not a visual redesign: same structure, spacing, and Heed. Only words and one small trust row are added.
