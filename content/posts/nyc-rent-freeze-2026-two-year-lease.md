# NYC Rent Freeze 2026: Why the 2-Year Lease Is Suddenly the Smart Move

**On June 25, 2026, the NYC Rent Guidelines Board voted 7 to 1 to freeze rents at 0% for BOTH 1-year and 2-year leases on rent-stabilized units, for lease terms that begin between October 1, 2026 and September 30, 2027. It is the first 2-year freeze in the board's history. If your stabilized lease renews in that window, signing the 2-year option locks 0% for two full years.**

Most renters have a rule of thumb in their head: the 2-year lease always costs more. This cycle, that rule is wrong. Here is what changed, who it covers, and the one date that decides whether it applies to you.

## What exactly did the board approve?

Every year the Rent Guidelines Board sets the maximum increase a landlord can apply to a rent-stabilized lease renewal. For the 2026 to 2027 cycle the board approved:

- **1-year renewal: 0%**
- **2-year renewal: 0%**

Past freezes only ever covered the 1-year option, and the 2-year always carried a higher rate. A freeze on both is new. The vote delivered on Mayor Mamdani's campaign pledge, and he had appointed 6 of the board's 9 members.

**What to do:** If you are stabilized and your renewal falls in the window, do not assume the 2-year costs more this time. It does not.

## Which leases does the freeze cover?

Two conditions, both required:

- The unit is **rent-stabilized** (roughly a million NYC units are).
- The new lease term **begins between October 1, 2026 and September 30, 2027.**

The date that matters is when the lease term starts, not when you sign it. A renewal signed in August 2026 that starts November 1, 2026 is covered. A renewal that starts September 1, 2026 falls under the previous cycle's rates.

**What to do:** Find the start date on your renewal offer before anything else. That single date decides which cycle's numbers apply.

## Why is the 2-year lease the smart move this cycle?

Because it locks 0% for two years instead of one, and it protects you from whatever the board votes next.

Nobody knows what the 2027 board will approve. If you take the 1-year at 0%, you are back at the table in 2027 facing that unknown. If you take the 2-year at 0%, you have flat rent through 2028. The usual price of that safety was a higher 2-year rate. This cycle the safety is free.

**What to do:** Unless you plan to leave the apartment within a year, take the 2-year option.

## My landlord's renewal offer shows an increase. Is that legal?

If the unit is stabilized and the term starts in the freeze window, an increase above 0% is not allowed. Renters accept illegal increases all the time simply because they did not know the number for their cycle.

A renter in Flatbush asked exactly this on Reddit: *"should I just take this increase and resign? just need a sanity check on if we think 5% is a fair increase."* The crowd debated whether 5% was fair. The real first question was whether the unit was stabilized, because if it was, the legal number for the next cycle is zero.

**What to do:** First confirm the unit is stabilized (request the rent history from [HCR](https://hcr.ny.gov/ask-us-question-or-request-rental-history), it is free). Then check the lease start date against the window. If both line up and the offer shows an increase, point at the RGB order in writing.

## What if I'm not sure my apartment is stabilized?

Landlords do not always volunteer it. Signs that point to stabilized: the building has 6 or more units and was built before 1974, your lease includes a stabilization rider, or the building got a tax break like 421-a. The certain way to know is your rent history from HCR, which shows the unit's registration.

**What to do:** Request the rent history before your renewal deadline. It settles the question and sometimes reveals past overcharges too.

## Common questions

### Does the 2026 rent freeze apply to market-rate apartments?

No. The freeze covers rent-stabilized units only. Market-rate increases are set by the lease and, in many buildings, limited by the Good Cause Eviction law instead.

### My renewal starts September 2026. Do I get the freeze?

No. The freeze covers lease terms beginning October 1, 2026 through September 30, 2027. A term starting before October 1 falls under the previous guideline. If you can lawfully shift your renewal start into the window, that changes the math.

### Is a 2-year lease riskier if I might move out?

Breaking any lease has costs, so a 2-year is a real commitment. If moving within a year is likely, the 1-year at 0% is the cleaner fit. If you are staying, the 2-year locks the freeze through 2028.

### Can my landlord refuse to renew because of the freeze?

Not for a stabilized tenant. Stabilization includes the right to a renewal lease. A landlord who suddenly "needs the unit" after a freeze vote deserves skepticism and a call to HCR.
